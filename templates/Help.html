1.Brief introduction

PTM (Post translational modification) of proteins has two regulatory roles on LLPS (Liquid-Liquid Phase Separation)1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8: 1)promotion and 2)inhibition. All PTMPhaSe data were curated manually from literatures. The literatures mining was performed via three ways: (1) retrieve LLPS records and associated literatures from 8 well-curated PTM databases (UniProt, PhosphoSitePlus, dbPTM, EPSD, CPLM, PTMD, iPTMnet, BIOGRID); (2) retrieve PTM records and associated literatures from 5 well-curated LLPS databases (PhaSepDB, DrLLPS, LLPSDB, PhaSePro, RNAgranuleDB); (3) retrieve relevant literature in PubMed (before July, 2024) by key words and combinations: liquid-liquid phase separation, phase separation, phase transition, Cajal body, stress granule, P-body, processing body, nuage, P granule, nuclear speckle, PTM, post translational modification, modification, phosphorylation, acetylation, methylation, SUMOylation, ubiquitylation, glycosylation. Then, we checked the full-text of above nearly 6,000 papers carefully to obtain complete experimental evidence, which included upstream enzymes, PTM sites and types, effects, LLPS regions, LLPS partners, detection methods, membraneless organelles (MLOs) and associated diseases.

3.Browse

Users can easily specify Organisms, PTM or MLOs to retrieve the results.


4.Search

PTMPhaSe database provides two search ways for users: 1)Quick search; 2)Advanced search.
The hit results will be shown in the tabular format. And detailed results can be obtained by clicking "Show" buttons.


5.Results

Click the "Show" button, the detailed results will be shown in a new page, which includes four sections:

(1)Overview, which provides protein basic information, protein structure and external links.


(2)Protein features. Disorder analysis: Exceed 0.5 means disorder.


(3)PTM on Liquid-Liquid Phase Separation, which be composed of three parts: complete experimental evidence, structure and site annotation.


(4)High-quality validated Protein-Protein Interactions


6.Statistics

Users can easily obtain intuitive statistics of PTMPhaSe database, including Organisms, MLOs, PTM, Proteins and Literature.
